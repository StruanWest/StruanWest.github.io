ABOUT ME

When I was young, I would spend most of my time playing games on the family computer before my brother was given a PlayStation 2 for his birthday. I would end up taking it over and spending a lot of time on games like Simpsons Hit & Run. It's around this time where my love of video games really blossmed, leading me to pursue creating my own games.

In 2018 I left secondary school which is when I signed up to join the UHI. My course taught me how to make websites, pass data around the internet and eventually, how to make games. So far I have developed, two 2D games, one 3D game and one VR game.

THE UNRELIABLE SAT NAV

This was my entry for the Game Jam 192: Episode 4. The theme was "The Unreliable Narrator". I then interpreted the narrator being someone who told you what to do or where to go, hence "The Unreliable Sat Nav".

The music paired with the Sat Nav's commentery was used to intentionally irritate the player. I felt this would work with the annoying sat nav without frustrating the player to the point where they want to quit. I also added multiple endings (as indicated by the stars on the menu screen).

This game was made using the Unity engine.

BOWLING VR GAME

This was my first time creating a VR game. Again, this was a college project, so I decided to try something simple while also adding in a personal twist. Instead of just simple bowling, I added in game tools which could be used in various ways to knock down the pins, including a golf club and gun.

This was made using the Unity engine and a Meta Quest 2 headset. In the end, I think it's a fun little game and I plan to explore VR more with other projects in future.
